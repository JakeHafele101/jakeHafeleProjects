Learning Blender
Blender is something I have been interested in for a while since I have gotten into 3D modeling. Before this, my only experience modeling was with Fusion 360 to help design and 3D print parts for my projects. I wanted to get into a different type of modeling, that would help me design more detailed 3D prints or renders that were just pretty to look at. I always feel like I have a lot of different ideas for how I want projects or things to look like, and I think Blender will give me a great opportunity at showing other people what I want to create.

So far, I have been mainly relying on online sources like youtube to help me create and render models in blender. There are plenty of great online tutorials on Youtube including Blender Guru's tutorial on how to make a coffee and doughnut. I have also been keeping a small notebook to jot down some small "hacks" or tricks that I learn along the way, just to make things easier to reference once I can stand on my own two feet with the software. I hope that I keep working on blender to find my niche in some fun things that I want to create. Right now I have been bouncing between many different techniques and features just to get an understanding of everything that the program has to offer. More to come soon!
